# Project Summary

## Methodology

The analysis combined historical Hyperliquid trading data with the Bitcoin Fear & Greed Index to study how market sentiment influences trader behavior and performance.

The datasets were cleaned, timestamps were standardized, and both datasets were merged at the daily level. Key metrics such as trader profitability, trade frequency, average position size, and directional trading behavior were analyzed across different market sentiment conditions.

Additional trader segmentation analysis was performed to compare:
- frequent vs infrequent traders
- consistent winners vs other traders
- top-performing traders under different market conditions

---

## Key Insights

The analysis revealed that trader behavior changes significantly across sentiment conditions.

Moderate Fear and Greed periods demonstrated the highest trading activity and stronger profitability compared to extreme sentiment phases. Top-performing traders showed disciplined risk management by reducing position sizes during Extreme Fear and Extreme Greed conditions.

Trader segmentation analysis showed that infrequent traders achieved higher average profitability than highly active traders, suggesting that selective participation may outperform excessive trading activity.

Additionally, traders with higher win rates did not necessarily achieve the highest average profitability, highlighting the importance of balancing consistency with effective risk-reward management.

---

## Strategy Recommendations

1. Reduce trade exposure during highly emotional market phases such as Extreme Fear and Extreme Greed.

2. Favor moderate trend conditions over emotionally overheated markets.

3. Follow disciplined position sizing patterns similar to top-performing traders, who showed smaller average trade sizes during extreme sentiment conditions and larger exposure during more stable market phases.

4. Avoid excessive trading activity and prioritize higher-conviction setups.

5. Evaluate trading performance using both profitability and risk-reward efficiency instead of relying solely on win rate.
